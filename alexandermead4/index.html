Hello and welcome

My name is Alexander Mead and I am a cosmologist currently holding a CITA post-doctoral fellowship and working at the University of British Columbia in Vancouver, Canada. My main interest is in large-scale structure formation and evolution; I am particularly interested in how both real- and redshift-space non-linear structure formation is influenced by 'beyond-standard-cosmological-model' physics, such as baryonic feedback, massive neutrinos, dark energy and modified gravitational physics.

Science

I interested in how non-linear structure formation can be understood using inspiration from cosmological simulations. These simulations are extremely useful, but are too expensive to be run for every possible cosmological model under consideration. I have worked on methods to alter the cosmology of a previously run simulation by rescaling length and time units and modifying halo structure. In other work I looked at using the halo model to produce accurate matter power spectra; the code generated during this work can be found here.
